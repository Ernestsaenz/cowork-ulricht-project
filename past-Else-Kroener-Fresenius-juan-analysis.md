## III. Scientific and Biomedical Content Analysis

### Public Health Context

**Primary challenge:** Healthcare access in Myanmar, particularly in rural/remote and conflict-affected areas. The February 2021 military coup triggered mass displacement, collapse of facility-based health services, and escalating armed conflict. The project targets populations served by ethnic health organizations in Karen State (southeast), Kachin State (north), and Karenni State.

### Intervention and Technology

**Telekyanmar (TLKM)** is an established telemedicine program that has conducted **190,000+ teleconsultations** since 2021, with **307 volunteer clinicians** and coverage across **322 of 330 townships**. The EKFS project scales this existing infrastructure through:

1. **Mobile clinic tele-touring** — 2 teams per EHO, 2 visits/month, targeting 10,800 total patient consultations and 1,800 specialist consultations by 2027
2. **MHPSS training** — 60 healthcare providers trained in mental health and psychosocial support; 180 health promotion sessions at mobile clinics
3. **Data security capacity-building** — 60 providers trained in secure data management
4. **Multilingual health promotion** — 36 materials in Karen, Kachin, Karenni languages plus Burmese

**Technical features:** Offline-capable mobile apps, SMS-based services, solar-powered devices, encrypted communication channels, Facebook-based health education outreach.

### Population and Beneficiaries

- **Direct beneficiaries:** 10,800 patients (consultations); 60+ healthcare providers (training)
- **67% female beneficiaries** (consistent with demographic patterns)
- **Target:** Conflict-affected, displaced, ethnic minority populations in three states

### Study Design

This is **not a research study per se** but an implementation project with rigorous M&E:
- Logframe with quantitative indicators (consultation counts, training completions, material production)
- Mid-term audit planned at month 18 (by BDO regional branch)
- Satisfaction surveys (baseline: 86% patient/provider satisfaction from existing TLKM operations)

### Strengths

- **Proven operational track record:** 190,000 consultations, 86% satisfaction — this is not a pilot but a scale-up
- **Experienced PI:** Dr. Kuch has 30+ years research, 100+ publications, prior EKFS-funded work (snakebite, Laos), 20+ years Myanmar engagement since 2003
- **Precedent for sustainability:** Dr. Kuch's Bangladesh telemedicine project (FlutNetz) successfully transitioned to government (National Poison Centre)
- **Risk-aware:** Explicitly addresses conflict, infrastructure, and organizational risks with technical mitigations (offline capability, solar power, SMS backup)
- **Gender integration:** 60.8% female providers in TLKM; gender focal point offering regular equality sessions

### Weaknesses/Risks

- **Fragile context:** Ongoing armed conflict, military surveillance, electricity/internet interruptions
- **Two partner confirmations pending** (KPICT, IEC) due to natural disaster delays
- **Sustainability depends on political stabilization** — federal Ministry funding assumed post-conflict
- **M&E capacity unclear** at EHO level — limited detail on baseline monitoring systems
- **No clinical outcome measurement** beyond consultation counts — quality of care, diagnostic accuracy, treatment outcomes not tracked

## V. Strategic Interpretation

### Project Goals

**Three-tiered objective:**
1. **Immediate:** Scale telemedicine access to 10,800 patients in 3 conflict-affected ethnic areas over 36 months
2. **Medium-term:** Build formal capacity in EHOs for telemedicine, MHPSS, and data security
3. **Long-term:** Model sustainable telemedicine integration in fragile post-conflict settings; pathway for government health system handover

### Maturity Stage

**Stage 3–4 (Implementation at scale)**
- Stage 1 (Concept): 2021 — Telekyanmar founded
- Stage 2 (Pilot): 2021–2023 — 190,000+ consultations, 86% satisfaction demonstrated
- Stage 3 (Expansion): Current — EKFS funding to scale across Karen, Kachin, Karenni via formal EHO partnerships
- Stage 4 (Sustainability): Post-project — Government handover when political stability returns

### Biggest Barriers

1. Conflict escalation/displacement preventing mobile clinic access
2. Internet/electricity infrastructure disruption
3. EHO organizational capacity for M&E and reporting
4. Political transition uncertainty affecting sustainability
5. Staff turnover and burnout in conflict zones

### Strongest Selling Points

1. **Proven impact before funding request** — TLKM is already saving lives
2. **Exact match to funder call** — Digital Health in Developing Countries
3. **Precedent for sustainability handover** — Bangladesh FlutNetz success
4. **Gender/equity integration as core design element** (not add-on)
5. **Risk-aware, honest about challenges** — builds reviewer trust
